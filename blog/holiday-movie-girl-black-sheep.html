The Holiday Movie Girl And The Black Sheep Nobody Listened To

-

8 min read

-
-
- -
-

There is a character in that movie "Holiday in Handcuffs" that messed me up a little.

- -

Not because it was meant to be deep. Because under all the chaos, she was weirdly accurate.

- -

She is talkative, scattered, impulsive. She makes terrible choices under pressure. She wants so badly to be accepted by her family that she does the wildest thing just to avoid disappointing them.

- -

If you have ever been the black sheep, you recognize her.

- -

She tells her parents she loves them, but if it is her or them, she picks herself. She is emotional and dramatic, but everything she does has a reason. She is trying to explain constantly, because nobody in her life actually understands where she is coming from.

- -
-

She says "I do not think you even see me."

-

That line hit harder than all the slapstick comedy.

-
- -

Because that is what it feels like when you grow up in a family that only sees the version of you they made up.

- -

You are the problem child, the overly sensitive one, the one who never quite "gets it together."

- -

Meanwhile you are actually the one seeing the cracks in the family image. The one feeling the tension everyone else pretends is not there. The one trying to hold it together without any guide.

- -

So you cope how you can.

- -

You over explain. You over share. You try to fix everything for everyone. You make choices that look insane from the outside, but are just you desperately trying to avoid another scene, another disappointment, another lecture.

- -

People laugh at characters like that. Some of us see our whole nervous system on the screen.

- -
-

The part that hurts is not that she is messy. It is that her mess is all anyone sees.

-

They do not see the black sheep who actually has a strong sense of justice.

-

They do not see the daughter who has been screaming silently for years "Stop loving the version of me in your head and see the version of me who is right here."

-
- -

If you see yourself in her, hear this.

- -

You are not crazy for wanting to be seen. You are not selfish for choosing yourself in a family that only cared about the role you played.

- -

It is okay if the only people who ever really understand you end up being strangers online, fictional characters, random friends who come later.

- -

You are not the joke character. You are the one who had to become their own witness.
